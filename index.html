About Our Group

Runners With Heart is a supportive post-cardiac-rehab community born from UHN’s cardiac rehabilitation program. Led by program graduates and inspired by Dr. Paul Oh, the group meets regularly to walk, run, ride, and connect, with new rehab graduates invited to join. It blends gentle exercise, social support, and heart-health awareness, helping members stay active, monitor progress, and thrive after a cardiac event.

Rehab Peer Support Groups

  • Graduates of cardiac rehab programs have the opportunity to maintain their healthy living prescriptions with the help of other graduates in peer support groups. There are two groups that started at the Rumsey Road Rehab Centre that currently meet virtually (using zoom) every month.

    The Cardiac Healthy Living Peer Support Group meets on the third Tuesday, 6:30 to 8:00 pm. Another, the Stress Reduction Graduate Group, meets on the third Thursday from 1:30 to 3:00 pm.

    At the meetings, a portion of time is set for sharing information on resources for managing our health and other topics of interest. Most of the meeting is for each person to share how they are keeping up with managing their health and wellbeing—exercising, walking/running, weight resistance, diet, managing stress.

    All sharing is in confidence. The aim is to be free to express concerns that we might have and find support in this group because we have similar lived experiences.

    If you would like more information or to join the Tuesday evening group, contact Emil Boychuk, emil.boychuk@gmail.com or call 416-476-8790. For the Thursday afternoon group, contact Lee Schacter, sschacter@rogers.com or call 416-464-9096.

YouTube Channels

  • Health-e University

    Health e-University, created by the UHN Cardiovascular Prevention & Rehabilitation Program at the Toronto Rehabilitation Institute, provides global access to trusted education on heart disease and diabetes management. The platform features dedicated, actionable resources through CardiacCollege.ca and DiabetesCollege.ca to help users understand medications and implement lifestyle changes. More information is available through the UHN Cardiovascular Prevention & Rehabilitation Program.

  • Heart Health for Life

    The Cardiac Rehab Alumni Channel, curated by the Heart Health for Life Team, provides free patient education videos, playlists, and live webcasts to support graduates and their families. It serves as a vital digital resource for continuing education, exercise, and self-management, featuring recordings of Take C.H.A.R.G.E. lectures.

  • The HERACLES Study

    The HERACLES Health Webinars platform offers educational resources and supports a five-year KITE Research Institute study tracking 1,000 cardiovascular patients. This research analyzes continuous Apple Watch data alongside in-clinic checkups to develop personalized, remote cardiac rehabilitation models. Read more at UHN.

  • University of Ottawa Heart Institute

    This premier institution is Canada's largest heart health centre specialized in cardiovascular disease treatment, research, and prevention.
